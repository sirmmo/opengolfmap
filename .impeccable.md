# opengolfmap — Design Context

## Users

People who appreciate that this is **OSM-derived**, not the millionth golf-app: cartographers, OSM editors, data-curious developers, and a small slice of golfers who happen to love maps. They can read raw tags, they value provenance (fetch dates, ISO codes, license attribution), and they will spend ten minutes panning across a country looking at fairway shapes for fun.

Primary context: desktop, deliberate browsing — not "I need the next tee time in 5 minutes." Phone is secondary, used for planning a trip or showing a friend.

## Brand Personality

Three words: **earthy · pastoral · slow**.

This is a **field guide**, not a SaaS dashboard or a sports app. The right physical reference is a 1970s hardback nature atlas, a letterpress trail manual, a pressed-leaf scrapbook with hand-set captions. Calm, generous margins, deliberate pace, transparent about its sources. The OSM heritage is shown — fetch dates and ISO tags surface as small typeset details — but never as a "developer aesthetic" of monospaced terminal-everything.

Voice: confident, quiet, slightly old-fashioned in the typeset sense. Captions over labels. Italic asides over tooltips.

## Aesthetic Direction

- **Theme:** default light, with a light/dark toggle. Light mode is **cream parchment**, not stock white. Dark mode is **midnight forest** — deep desaturated green-black with cream text.
- **Palette:** pressed-leaf greens (moss, oxidized copper), warm naturals (cream, oat, antique paper), terracotta and burnt sienna as rare accents, faded watercolour blue for water hazards. Sand bunkers as warm sandstone, not lemon yellow. No vivid sport-app green.
- **Type:** **Vollkorn** (warm German "wholegrain" serif, multiple weights + italics) for everything from masthead to body — one voice. **Cutive Mono** (typewriter face) for OSM data captions and ISO/tag micro-labels only. No third font, no Inter/Plex/DM/Fraunces/Newsreader/Cormorant/Crimson/Playfair.
- **Surfaces:** paper, not chrome. Hairline 1px rules. No drop shadows. No glassmorphism. No border-left accent stripes (banned). Subtle paper-grain overlay on the map and sidebar to read as printed cartography rather than "Maplibre demo".
- **Layout:** asymmetric, generous margins, hierarchy through space and weight rather than dividers. The list is set like an atlas index — name, country, hairline, tiny tag annotations beneath.
- **Map:** the basemap reads as aged paper (warm tint + grain), so our golf rendering is the hero. Golf layers use botanical-illustration colors, not sport-app saturation.

## Anti-References

Explicitly NOT this:
- **Generic dashboard SaaS** — Linear/Stripe-style cards, blue-purple gradients, sidebar-with-rounded-search, drop-shadow detail panels.
- **Generic Mapbox/Maplibre demo** — default Positron + floating search bar + a "made with Mapbox" attribution. We use Positron tiles but treat the canvas, not leave it stock.
- **Country-club kitsch** — tartan, mahogany panels, gold trim, "Old English" type, snobby clubhouse vibe.
- **Golf-app cliché** — bright chartreuse heroes, big distance numbers, gradient swooshes — the 18Birdies/Garmin look.

## Design Principles

1. **OSM transparency, pastoral form.** The OSM heritage is surfaced through small typeset details (tags, ISO codes, fetch dates in Cutive Mono) sitting inside warm paper-like surfaces — not as a "developer aesthetic" with terminal monospace everywhere.
2. **Paper, not chrome.** Surfaces are cream parchment with hairline rules. No drop shadows, no glassmorphism, no accent stripes, no rounded-rectangle SaaS cards. Hierarchy comes from typography, weight, and generous space.
3. **Slow, deliberate motion.** Transitions are unhurried (400–700 ms with ease-out-quart). Map fly-tos are gentle. No springs, no bounces, no micro-jitter. The product moves at the pace of someone leafing through a book.
4. **One typographic voice.** Vollkorn does everything from masthead to body — weight/size/italic carries hierarchy. Cutive Mono is reserved for OSM data captions only. Two faces, total.
5. **The map is the artifact, not a widget.** The basemap is treated (warm tint, subtle grain) so it reads as cartography. Golf features are rendered with botanical-illustration colors — pressed-leaf greens, sandstone, faded watercolour blue.
